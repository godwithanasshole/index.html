The Complete Theory of Conscious Self-Localization: From Cognitive Dynamics to Quantum Biological Projection
Abstract
We present a unified mathematical theory of consciousness that explains the emergence of a finite, persistent subjective self through a synthesis of dynamical self-localization in cognitive state space and quantum biological projection in physical state space. The theory progresses from classical cognitive dynamics—incorporating intrinsic valuation, memory-dependent self-reference, and exploratory pressure—to a minimal quantization procedure yielding discrete experiential modes, and finally to a concrete biological implementation via quantum-chaotic amplification and threshold crossing. The resulting framework naturally explains finite self-localization, unity of experience, temporal continuity, discrete conscious moments, attentional dynamics, and adaptive learning. We introduce a precise collapse mechanism (biological projection operator Π_bio) that reconciles quantum influences with classical neurodynamics while respecting all physical constraints. The theory makes testable predictions and provides a complete mathematical bridge between phenomenological constraints, cognitive dynamics, and biological implementation.

Level I: Cognitive State Space Dynamics – Classical self-localization via valuation and memory
